Shifts in Alignment: A Timeline of EU-U.S. Voting Patterns Since 2000

The relationship between the European Union (EU) and the United States (US) has been a defining force in global diplomacy. One of the key arenas where this relationship is tested is the United Nations (UN), where both entities regularly vote on a variety of resolutions. But how often do their voting patterns diverge, and how has this evolved over time?

This data story examines the voting behaviour of the EU and the US within the UN over the past 25 years, using the GINA Diplomatic tool to analyse and visualize the divergence of their voting records. By exploring the data, we uncover trends in how closely the EU and the US have differed on major resolutions, and how (geo)political events have influenced their voting behaviour. From international crises to shifting political dynamics, these moments have shaped their diplomatic strategies and, in turn, their voting tendencies within the UN.

Diplomatic

The GINA Diplomatic Tool seeks to revolutionize the analysis of diplomatic behavior by combining voting and speech analysis. It utilizes advanced metrics to evaluate state-specific voting patterns and alignments over time. Additionally, the tool incorporates Natural Language Processing (NLP) techniques to examine the sentiment and tone of diplomatic speeches, offering deeper insights into nations' diplomatic positions.

By integrating voting records with speech transcripts and applying a structured three-tier methodology, GINA delivers a comprehensive and precise approach to understanding global diplomacy and geopolitical dynamics.
